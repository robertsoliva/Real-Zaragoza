# Real Zaragoza — Club History

> **Status:** living document, last updated 2026-06-24
> Atomic, sourced facts only. Update in place as new facts are verified; do not let entries go stale — when a fact changes (promotion, relegation, a new title), edit the relevant section and bump the date above.

## Identity

- **Full name:** Real Zaragoza, S.A.D.
- **Founded:** 18 March 1932, from the merger/dissolution of Iberia SC and Real Zaragoza Club Deportivo (the two earlier Zaragoza clubs).
- **Stadium:** La Romareda, Zaragoza.
- **Nickname:** *Los Maños* / the "Zaragocistas".

## Seasons played (1932–2026)

Across 91 league seasons since foundation:

| Category | Seasons |
|---|---|
| Primera División | 58 |
| Segunda División | 29 |
| Tercera División | 4 |

- Spent most of its history in the top flight, but has **not played in Primera División since being relegated in 2013** — 12 consecutive seasons in Segunda before being relegated again in 2026 (see below).
- **Best-ever league finish:** runner-up (2nd place) in La Liga, 1974–75 season.
- **"Los Magníficos" era:** beginning in the 1960–61 season, a golden generation that produced the club's first major trophies and its strongest period of European competitiveness.

## Honours / Major titles (9 total)

| Title | Count | Season(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Copa del Rey | 6 | 1963–64, 1965–66, 1985–86, 1993–94, 2000–01, 2003–04 |
| Recopa de Europa (UEFA Cup Winners' Cup) | 1 | 1994–95 (beat Arsenal in the final, on penalties, in Paris) |
| Supercopa de España | 1 | 2004–05 |
| Copa de Ferias (Inter-Cities Fairs Cup) | 1 | 1963–64 |

Notable final: the 2003–04 Copa del Rey, won 3–2 after extra time against Real Madrid.

## Relegations / structural decline

- **2013:** relegated from Primera División. Has not returned since.
- **24 May 2026:** relegation to **Primera Federación (Primera RFEF)** — the third tier — confirmed after a 1–1 draw away to UD Las Palmas (penultimate matchday of the 2025–26 Segunda División season). This is the club's first time in the third tier in **77 years**, and is described in Aragonese press as the biggest institutional/sporting crisis in the club's 94-year history. Huesca and Cultural Leonesa were relegated alongside Zaragoza that same matchday.
