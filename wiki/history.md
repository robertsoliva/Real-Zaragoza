# Real Zaragoza — Club History

> **Status:** living document, last updated 2026-06-24. Sources of truth for this page: [realzaragoza.com](https://www.realzaragoza.com/) and [Spanish Wikipedia](https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Zaragoza) — where other sources disagree with these two, these two win.

## TL;DR

- Founded 1932 in Zaragoza; 58 seasons in Primera División, 29 in Segunda out of 91 total league seasons
- 9 major titles: 6 Copas del Rey/España, 1 Recopa de Europa (1995 "Héroes de París"), 1 Supercopa (2004), 1 Copa de Ferias (1964)
- Best league finish: 2nd in La Liga, 1974–75 ("Los Zaraguayos" era)
- Not in Primera División since 2013; further relegated to Primera RFEF (3rd tier) in May 2026
- Stadium: La Romareda (1957–2025) demolished; club now at provisional Ibercaja Estadio; new Ibercaja Romareda expected ~2027

## Identity

- **Full name:** Real Zaragoza, S.A.D.
- **Founded:** 1932. The founding act was drafted 15 March and signed 18 March 1932 by ten directors, under the name "Zaragoza Foot-ball Club."
- **Nickname:** *Los Maños* / "Zaragocistas".

## Founding

The club was formed out of a local football crisis: by late 1931 the city's existing clubs were collapsing or fading, and local leaders concluded "solamente un equipo debía abanderar a la ciudad" (only one club should represent the city). Predecessor/contributing clubs:

- **Iberia Sport Club** — founded 24 March 1917 by students of the El Salvador school, played in black and yellow stripes. Was in the second division but facing relegation at the time of the merger.
- **Zaragoza** (Club Deportivo) — had itself absorbed earlier teams Stadium and Fuenclara, but dissolved by late 1931.
- **Real Sociedad Atlética Stadium** — an earlier merger of several entities that had been granted a royal title by the King.
- **Patria Aragón** — another local club that faded out around the same time.

The new club took blue and white from the Aragonese Football Federation. **José María Gayarre** (from Iberia) became the first president; **Elías Sauca** was player-coach. The first match was played 20 March 1932 against Valladolid, won by four goals, with **Rolloso** scoring the club's first-ever goal.

## Seasons played (1932–2026)

Across 91 league seasons since foundation:

| Category | Seasons |
|---|---|
| Primera División | 58 |
| Segunda División | 29 |
| Tercera División | 4 |

- Spent most of its history in the top flight, but has **not played in Primera División since being relegated in 2013** — 12 consecutive seasons in Segunda before being relegated again in 2026 (see below).
- **Best-ever league finish:** runner-up (2nd place) in La Liga, 1974–75 season.
- **"Los Magníficos" era:** beginning in the 1960–61 season, a golden generation that produced the club's first major trophies and its strongest period of European competitiveness.

## Honours / Major titles (9 total, per the club's own "Nuestros trofeos")

The Spanish Cup has been won 6 times — it was officially named **Copa de España** in 1964/1966, and **Copa del Rey** from the 1976 restoration of the monarchy onward; both names refer to the same competition.

| Title | Count | Season(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Spanish Cup (Copa de España / Copa del Rey) | 6 | 1964 (as Copa de España), 1966 (as Copa de España), 1986, 1994, 2001, 2004 |
| Recopa de Europa (UEFA Cup Winners' Cup) | 1 | 1995 |
| Supercopa de España | 1 | 2004 |
| Copa de Ferias (Inter-Cities Fairs Cup) | 1 | 1964 |

Notable finals:
- **1995 Recopa de Europa:** beat Arsenal 2–1 after extra time in Paris — Nayim's lob from the halfway line in the 120th minute is one of the most famous goals in the club's history ("Héroes de París"). **Not** a penalty shootout.
- **2003–04 Copa del Rey:** won 3–2 after extra time against Real Madrid.

## Notable eras

- **"Los Magníficos" (1960s):** forward line of Canario, Santos, Marcelino, Villa and Lapetra; reached four Copa finals.
- **"Los Zaraguayos" (1970s):** squad built around Uruguayan and Paraguayan players; produced the club's best-ever league finish, **runner-up in La Liga, 1974–75**.
- **"Héroes de París" (1995):** the Recopa-winning squad (see above).
- **"Dark Era" (2006–2014):** under the Bandrés–Iglesias administration, the club churned through roughly 129 players and 10 managers and suffered two Segunda División relegations during this stretch.

## Relegations from Primera División

- **2001–02:** first relegation, ending a run of 24 consecutive seasons in Primera División.
- **2007–08:** relegated again after only one prior season in Segunda; bounced straight back up via promotion in 2008–09.
- **2012–13:** third relegation in 11 years. The club has not returned to Primera División since.

## 2026: relegation out of professional football

- **24 May 2026:** relegation to **Primera Federación (Primera RFEF)** — the third tier — confirmed after a 1–1 draw away to UD Las Palmas (penultimate matchday of the 2025–26 Segunda División season; the club finished 22nd, bottom of the table). This is the club's first time in the third tier in **77 years**, and is described in Aragonese press as the biggest institutional/sporting crisis in the club's 94-year history. Huesca and Cultural Leonesa were relegated alongside Zaragoza that same matchday.

## Stadium

- **La Romareda** was the club's home from its construction (1957) until the end of the 2024–25 season, with a final capacity of 33,608. It was fully demolished during summer 2025 to make way for a rebuild.
- The club currently plays at the **Ibercaja Estadio**, a provisional, modular stadium (Spain's first) seating 20,071, built in roughly 26 weeks and inaugurated 24 July 2025.
- The new stadium on the same site — sponsor-named **Ibercaja Romareda** through 2035 — is expected to be completed around mid-to-late 2027; capacity could be expanded to 42,119 if Zaragoza is awarded matches in the 2030 World Cup.
