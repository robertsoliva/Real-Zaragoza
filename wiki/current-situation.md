# Real Zaragoza — Current Situation (as of 2026-06-24)

> **Status:** living document, highly volatile right now — the club is mid-restructuring after relegation. Several roles below are explicitly unconfirmed/in flux; treat those lines as open questions, not facts, until a follow-up update closes them out.

## Sporting context

The club is coming off the **2025–26 Segunda División season**, which ended in relegation to **Primera RFEF** (third tier), confirmed 24 May 2026 — see `history.md`. The season was marked by three different head coaches and the dismissal of a sporting director mid-season. The club enters 2026–27 in its deepest institutional crisis in 94 years.

## Ownership / shareholding structure

Real Zaragoza is owned through the **Real Z LLC** holding structure. The current ownership group took control in **May 2022**.

- **Jorge Mas** — club president, principal figure of the controlling group alongside Juan Forcén.
- **Juan Forcén** — local businessman, co-controller alongside Mas.
- **Historic / minority stakeholders involved in 2025–26 boardroom moves:**
  - Pablo Jiménez de Parga — major shareholder, target of an attempted buyout.
  - Global Tavira — previously the majority partner within Real Z LLC (50.1%), being diluted out.
  - Ares Management — investment fund being removed from the shareholder structure.
  - Joseph Oughourlian — president of Grupo Prisa and owner of Racing Club de Lens; attempted to buy Jiménez de Parga's stake to become majority shareholder, but Mas/Forcén instead moved to consolidate control themselves.
- **June 2026 capital increase:** the board approved a €20M capital increase, the largest since the 2022 takeover, used to push Mas and Forcén toward majority control of Real Z LLC and to squeeze out Global Tavira and Ares.
- **Open / in progress:** Jorge Mas has signaled he may step back from the *day-to-day presidency* (reported ~12 May 2026) without giving up his shareholding weight. The club is, as of 23 June 2026, finalizing the creation of a **non-shareholding "institutional president"** (representative role) and a **new general director**, replacing outgoing general director **Fernando López** (departure agreed ~2 June 2026). Names for these two new posts are not yet confirmed publicly — **flag to revisit**.

### 2025–26 coaching carousel (for context)

1. **Gabi Fernández** — arrived March 2024, renewed through 2026 in summer 2025. Dismissed 12 October 2025 after a 4-2 loss to Almería with the team bottom of Segunda (6 points from 9 games); overall record at the club: 5 wins in 20 matches.
2. **Rubén Sellés** — appointed as the club's 22nd manager since the 2013 relegation from Primera. Dismissed 2 March 2026 alongside sporting director Txema Indias.
3. **David Navarro** — interim, then confirmed through the end of the season; oversaw the team during its relegation to Primera RFEF.
