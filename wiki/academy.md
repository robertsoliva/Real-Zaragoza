## Facilities

- **Ciudad Deportiva del Real Zaragoza** (Carretera de Valencia, km 8, Zaragoza) has operated since **1974** as the training base for the first team, **Deportivo Aragón** (the reserve team — see [current-situation.md](./current-situation.md)), and every lower category in the club's structure.
- Six football pitches (three natural grass, three artificial turf), two gymnasiums (one exclusively for the first team), plus dedicated spaces for coaching, medical and equipment staff.
- The facility also runs community-facing youth activity: the Torneo San Jorge, summer/winter camps, and the "Escuela de Fútbol Real Zaragoza."

## Categories

Men's youth structure (as organized into national/regional competition tiers):

- **Juvenil** (U-19) — competes in División de Honor Juvenil.
- **Cadete** (U-16) — Primera Cadete / División de Honor Cadete.
- **Infantil** (U-14) — Primera Infantil.
- **Alevín** (U-12) — Primera Alevín, also competes in the Copa Aragón.
- **Deportivo Aragón** sits above all of these as the bridge to the first team.

**Real Zaragoza Femenino** runs in parallel as the club's women's academy, with three teams spanning alevín-benjamín, cadete-infantil and a senior/"1ª-2ª Femenina" team; the women's football area is led by **Miki Álvarez**. The Zaragoza city council has separately part-funded grassroots and women's-academy football via a sponsorship agreement with the club (~€700,000).

## 2026 overhaul

The academy is in the middle of a deliberate relaunch, bundled into the same March 2026 shake-up that brought in **Lalo Arantegui** as sporting director and **Fran Gracia** as Technical Secretary (see [current-situation.md](./current-situation.md)):

- **Javier Garcés** was named the new **Director of the Youth Academy** ("director de la cantera"), reporting into Arantegui's sporting structure.
- Stated philosophy: prioritize **individual talent development** and build a single, club-wide playing identity, referred to internally as the "**ADN de juego**" (game DNA) — the same on-pitch identity meant to run from the youngest categories up through the first team.
- A genuinely novel retention mechanism, the "**contrato hucha**" (savings-account contract): the club secures a commitment from a young prospect, but the agreed economic compensation isn't paid out until the player turns 16 and signs his first professional contract. The explicit goal is to stop other clubs from poaching academy talent before that point, and to give families a reason to bet long-term on the club rather than cash out early.
- Arantegui has framed this as existential to the rebuild, not a side project: *"La cantera tiene que ser para nosotros el motor principal"* ("the academy has to be our main engine") — a direct response to the financial reality of operating in Primera RFEF (see [finances.md](./finances.md)), where developing rather than buying talent is also a budget necessity.

## Historical graduates ("canteranos")

Players confirmed to have come up through Real Zaragoza's own academy (not simply players who passed through the first team):

- **Ander Herrera** (b. 14 Aug 1989, Zaragoza) — academy product, debuted with the first team, left for Athletic Club in 2011 at age 22, went on to Manchester United, PSG and the Spain national team. As of June 2026, his return to Zaragoza — fifteen years after leaving, following the end of his Boca Juniors contract — was being actively negotiated as a marquee, symbolic signing for the rebuild in Primera RFEF. **Not yet officially confirmed at time of writing — flag to revisit.**
- **Jesús Vallejo** (academy product) — rated one of the best young Spanish defenders of his generation before joining Real Madrid; U-19 European champion (Greece, 2015) and U-21 European champion (Italy/San Marino, 2019).
- **Ángel Lafita** — academy product, went on to a long Primera/Segunda career.
- **Rubén "Cani" Gracia Calmache** (b. 1981, Zaragoza) — academy product, debuted 2002, key player in the club's promotion back to Primera and in the **2003–04 Copa del Rey** and **2004 Supercopa de España** wins (see [history.md](./history.md)).
- **Alberto Zapater** — debuted 2004–05 and went on to make 300+ official appearances for the first team, the standout one-club-man example of the academy pipeline in the modern era.

For a snapshot of how the pipeline looked in a single season: in **2018–19**, the official site counted ten academy products with first-team minutes — Álvaro Ratón, Julián Delmás, Daniel Lasure, Carlos Nieto, Alberto Zapater, Raúl Guti, Pep Biel, Alberto Soro, Jorge Pombo and Raí Nascimento. This is a dated example, not a current list — useful mainly to show the academy has reliably fed the first team across eras, not just in moments of financial necessity.

## Current academy products in the first team (2025–26 → 2026–27)

- **Iván Azón** — striker, 20 years old, emerged as starting No. 9 during 2025–26 (7 goals, 6 in the league) after Soberón's injury.
- **Francho Serrano** (Francho Serrano Gracia, b. 17 Oct 2001, Zaragoza) — right-midfielder/midfielder, Spain U-19/U-21 international, academy reference point and a candidate for the captaincy. As of June 2026 his contract was set to expire 30 June, and his renewal — balancing sporting value against the club's financial reality in Primera RFEF — was flagged as one of the key storylines of the summer rebuild.

Both players' contracts were due to end 30 June 2026 at the time of the last check — **confirm renewal status once pre-season squads are announced.**

## Why this matters for the current rebuild

With the club relegated to Primera RFEF and under financial strain (see [current-situation.md](./current-situation.md) and [finances.md](./finances.md)), home-grown players are not just sentimental — they're a budget lever: cheaper than transfers, and the explicit stated strategy ("la cantera... el motor principal") ties the academy directly to how the club intends to survive and rebuild in the third tier.
