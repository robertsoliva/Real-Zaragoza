# Real Zaragoza — Finances & Debt

> **Status:** living document, last updated 2026-06-24. ## TL;DR

- 2011 insolvency (€130M liabilities) under Agapito Iglesias — ordinary debt restructured but creditors still unpaid as late as 2020
- César Alierta's Fundación 2032 stabilized the club 2014–2022; Jorge Mas consortium took over April 2022
- €20M capital increase approved June 2026 (Mas + Forcén), post-relegation, to finance the Primera RFEF promotion push
- 2026–27 budget: ~€5–6M, among the highest in Primera RFEF; LaLiga's salary-limit framework no longer applies
- Ibercaja naming rights: 10-year deal (2025–2035), valued €10–19M depending on which division the club plays in

## The 2011 insolvency and its long tail

- Under **Agapito Iglesias**'s ownership (2006–2014, the "Dark Era" — see [history.md](./history.md)), the club's debt ballooned from roughly €50M to **€146M in five years**.
- In **June 2011**, Real Zaragoza SAD formally declared insolvency (**concurso de acreedores**), with liabilities of **€130M**, placed under the supervision of Zaragoza's Commercial Court No. 2.
- Restructuring brought the **ordinary debt** down to about **€27.2M** by 2020 (from an initial €91M in ordinary claims) — but the tail was long: in **July 2020**, the club admitted it could not make a scheduled **€2.44M** payment to roughly 300 creditors and asked the court to suspend that installment, citing COVID-era stadium closures (~€4.06M in estimated lost revenue). Nearly nine years after the 2011 declaration, some creditors dating back to 2018–2019 (€566,500 and €278,001 respectively) still hadn't been paid.

## The 2026 capital increase

- Following relegation to Primera RFEF, the board approved a capital increase of **~€20M** (reported as €20.2M by some outlets) in early June 2026, to be funded by **Jorge Mas and Juan Forcén** (per reporting; not named in the club's own statement).
- Stated purpose, per the club: provide "the necessary resources to face with maximum guarantees the challenge of returning to professional football" — i.e., funding the promotion push (transfers, wages). At least one outlet (2Playbook) frames part of the same move as cleaning up remaining creditor debt rather than purely sporting investment — **these two framings aren't necessarily exclusive, but it's worth tracking which one the money actually goes to.**

## Budget / wage bill

- **2025–26 (Segunda División):** LaLiga set Real Zaragoza's salary limit ("límite salarial") at **€11.301M**, slightly down from the prior season.
- **2026–27 (Primera RFEF):** initial club budget forecasts of **€5–6M** were reported as among the highest in the category — consistent with being a promotion favourite. Primera RFEF's own economic control rules cap total squad cost (players + technical staff) at **70% of each club's projected income**, rather than setting a flat league-wide limit the way LaLiga does for Segunda.
- Falling out of LaLiga's financial framework into Primera RFEF's also means losing LaLiga's broadcasting revenue share — a real financial hit layered on top of the sporting one, though no exact broadcasting-revenue figure for Primera RFEF has been verified yet for this page.

## Sponsorship & stadium financing

- **Ibercaja** holds the naming rights to both of the club's stadiums (the temporary **Ibercaja Estadio** and the permanent **Ibercaja Romareda**) under a 10-year deal (2025–2035), signed with **La Nueva Romareda SL** (the vehicle tied to the new stadium project, not the football club entity directly).
- Reported terms: an upfront/advance payment of **€10M**, plus an annual fee of **€1M per season in Segunda División or lower** and **€2M per season in Primera División**, with the total package valued at **€10–19M** over the deal's life depending on which division the club is in. **Open question: it's unclear from current sourcing whether Primera RFEF (third tier) falls under the "€1M" lower-tier rate or a different figure — not yet specified anywhere found.**
